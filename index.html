Hi, my name is Kai Denmark; thank you for visiting my web page :) I just want to take a moment to explain the purpose of this website and how it came about.

During Trump’s presidency, the refugee resettlement rate dropped sharply due to a much lower ceiling put on refugee acceptance and admission rates. The situation has always been bleak, but it got worse thanks to regulations and restrictions the former president put in place. So, I decided I wanted to do something about it. On this webpage you will find resources that will help you educate and enlighten you about these problems. There is also information on how to donate and take action. This will help organizations further their ability to help others that are struggling, because immigrants and refugees are suffering due to forced family separation, harsh restrictions (such as the zero tolerance policy), and a general sense of not being welcomed into this country. So, please take time to think about these people because they deserve a chance too. And, don't feel pressured to donate, you can help in other ways like spreading the word or writing a letter to your elected official.

Anyways, thank you for visiting my web page and have a great day! – Kai

Donating money or goods is another way you can support this cause. By giving a charitable contribution you provide the resources necessary to continue to help people in need. Although it's not required, if you do donate just know that a little goes a long way.

JFCS Collection Drive Information

I have also chosen Jewish Family & Community Services (JFCS) East Bay specifically because they have a refugee program and they have helped many families in need.

  • JFCS Refugee Resettlement Program

What to Donate:

  • Toiletries: newly purchased FULL SIZE shampoo, conditioner, soap, toothpaste, toothbrush, dental floss, lotion, and shaving items (These can be bagged together)
  • Small household items: newly purchased complete sets of dishes, utensils, mixing bowls, pots, pans, bakeware, microwave, toaster, mixer, crock pot, rice cooker, electric teapot, radio/alarm clock, vacuum, iron and ironing board (Please, no plastic or anything alcohol-related)
  • Cleaning supplies: newly purchased brooms, mops, vacuums, scrubbers, dustpans, buckets, and cleaning products including dish soap, sponges, laundry detergent, and Clorox/Windex.
  • Baby items: new packs of diapers and wipes, baby soap/shampoo/wash, strollers, car seats, booster seats, educational toys/games. If gently used, items need to be very well cleaned.
  • School supplies: backpacks, lunch boxes, pencils, pens, binders, lined paper, notebooks, markers, crayons, colored pencils, scissors, tape, staplers.
  • Anything else you think a immigrant or refugee family would need!
